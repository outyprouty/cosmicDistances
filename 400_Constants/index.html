Cosmic Distances: Constants

Universal Constants

Gravitational Constant

\(G = 6.67408 \times 10^{-11} \frac{N\cdot m^2}{kg^2}\) [1]

Speed of Light in Vacuum

\(c = 2.99792458 \times 10^{8} \frac{m}{s}\) [1]

Stefan-Boltzmann Constant

\(\sigma = 5.670373 \times 10^{-8} \frac{W}{m^2\cdot K}\) [1]

Pi

\(\pi = 3.14159 \approx \pi\) [Google]

Solar System Constants

Sun

Solar Mass

\(m_{Sun} = 1.99 \times 10^{30} kg\) [1]

Solar Radius (average)

\(r_{Sun} = 6.96 \times 10^{8} m\) [1]

Distance to Earth

\(d_{Sun} = 1.496 \times 10^{11} m\) [1]

Earth

Earth Mass

\(m_{Earth} = 5.9736 \times 10^{24} kg\) [1]

Earth Radius (average)

\(r_{Earth} = 6.376 \times 10^{6} m\) [1]

Distance to Earth

\(d_{Earth} = 0 m\) [1]

Orbital Period about Sun

\(T_{Earth,Sun} = 3.16 \times 10^7 s\) [1]

Moon

Moon Mass

\(m_{Moon} = 7.35 \times 10^{24} kg\) [1]

Moon Radius (average)

\(r_{Moon} = 1.74 \times 10^{6} m\) [1]

Distance to Earth

\(d_{Moon} = 3.84 \times 10^{8} m\) [1]

Orbital Period about Earth

\(T_{Moon,Earth} = 2.36 \times 10^6 s\) [1]

Particle Masses

Mass of Proton/Hydrogen Nucleus

\(m_p = 1.67262 \times 10^{-27} kg\) [1]

Mass of Neutron

\(m_n = 1.67492 \times 10^{-27} kg\) [1]

Mass of Electron

\(m_e = 9.10938 \times 10^{-31} kg\) [1]

Astronomical Numbers

Astronomical Unit (AU)

1 AU is the Mean Distance from the Earth to the Sun

\[1 AU = 1.496\times 10^8 kilometers\]

Light Year (ly)

1 Light Year (ly) is the Distance Traveled by Light in 1 Year

\[1 ly = 9.46x10^{12} kilometers\]
